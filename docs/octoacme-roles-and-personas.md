# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA/Testing Lead

### Role Summary
QA/Testing Leads define quality standards, design test strategies, and validate that features meet acceptance criteria and performance requirements before release.

### Responsibilities
- Define Definition of Done (DoD) testing requirements in collaboration with Product and Project leads
- Design and execute test plans, including unit, integration, E2E, and smoke tests
- Validate acceptance criteria are met before marking work as complete
- Identify and report quality issues with clear reproduction steps
- Partner with Developers on testability improvements and test automation
- Track test coverage metrics and quality trends

### Goals
- Ensure customer-facing features are reliable and performant
- Reduce post-release defects through early quality validation
- Build a culture of testability and quality ownership across teams

### Typical Communication
- Sprint planning and backlog refinement sessions
- QA sign-off gates in PR/code review workflows
- Quality dashboards and defect reports
- Weekly delivery syncs for quality status updates

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Collaborate on test automation, testability improvements, and bug verification
- **Product Managers**: Validate acceptance criteria and test coverage expectations
- **Project Managers**: Report quality status and track defect resolution timelines

---

## Stakeholders

### Role Summary
Stakeholders include business sponsors, executives, customer representatives, and other parties whose interests, decisions, or resources affect project success. They provide guidance, approvals, and business context.

### Responsibilities
- Define business requirements and success metrics
- Approve project charter and major milestones
- Provide feedback on priorities and trade-offs
- Support escalations and unblock resource constraints
- Validate solutions align with business objectives
- Communicate project status to broader organization

### Goals
- Ensure projects deliver measurable business value
- Reduce misalignment and rework due to unclear requirements
- Enable informed decision-making on scope and timeline trade-offs

### Typical Communication
- Project initiation and approval meetings
- Monthly stakeholder updates and demos
- Ad-hoc escalation and decision forums
- Release announcements and business impact summaries

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Managers**: Receive escalations and provide decisions on trade-offs
- **Product Managers**: Validate business requirements and success metrics
- **Developers**: Participate in demos and provide feedback on solutions

---

## Technical Architect

### Role Summary
Technical Architects design system solutions, ensure scalability and security, and guide engineering decisions to align with long-term technical strategy.

### Responsibilities
- Design technical solutions for complex features or system changes
- Review architectural decisions and identify technical risks
- Guide performance, scalability, and security design choices
- Mentor developers on technical best practices
- Identify and propose technical debt reduction initiatives
- Support capacity planning and infrastructure decisions

### Goals
- Build scalable, maintainable, and secure systems
- Reduce technical debt and prevent architecture erosion
- Enable faster feature delivery through thoughtful design

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and architecture decision records (ADRs)
- Technical design discussions during planning
- Code review collaboration on complex changes
- Technical risk discussions in weekly syncs

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Provide technical guidance and design review feedback
- **Project Managers**: Communicate technical risks and mitigation strategies
- **Security/Compliance Officers**: Collaborate on security architecture and compliance requirements

---

## Security/Compliance Officer

### Role Summary
Security/Compliance Officers ensure projects meet security standards, compliance requirements, and risk management practices.

### Responsibilities
- Identify and assess security and compliance risks
- Review designs and code for security vulnerabilities
- Define and enforce security requirements for new features
- Conduct security incident triage and response coordination
- Track compliance obligations and audit readiness
- Partner with teams on security training and best practices

### Goals
- Minimize security and compliance risks
- Build security into the development lifecycle from the start
- Enable fast incident response with clear runbooks and ownership

### Typical Communication
- Security design reviews and threat assessments
- Security scanning results and remediation guidance
- Incident response coordination and post-incident reviews
- Compliance audit and regulatory update briefings

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Technical Architects**: Collaborate on security architecture and design requirements
- **Developers**: Provide security guidance, code review feedback, and training
- **Project Managers**: Communicate security and compliance risks and escalate incidents
- **Stakeholders**: Provide compliance status and regulatory alignment updates

---

## Delivery Coordinator / Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Delivery Coordinators (or Scrum Masters in Agile contexts) facilitate team processes, remove blockers, and help teams maintain a sustainable pace. They support the Project Manager in day-to-day execution activities.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate daily standups and sprint ceremonies (planning, review, retrospective)
- Help identify and remove blockers that impede progress
- Track sprint velocity and burndown metrics
- Promote team health, psychological safety, and continuous improvement
- Coordinate with external teams and dependencies
- Maintain sprint board and backlog organization

### Goals
- Keep teams focused and productive
- Reduce process bottlenecks and cycle time
- Foster a culture of continuous improvement and team ownership

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint ceremonies
- Blocker escalations to Project Manager and Product Manager
- Burndown and velocity reports
- Retrospective facilitation and action item tracking

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Managers**: Report on team health, blockers, and sprint progress
- **Developers**: Remove impediments and facilitate collaboration
- **Product Managers**: Communicate sprint capacity and velocity trends
- **All Roles**: Support process adherence and team ceremonies
